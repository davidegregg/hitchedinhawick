Frequently Asked Questions

We're here to help answer your questions about our special day. If you can't find what you're looking for, please don't hesitate to get in touch!

What time should I arrive?

Please arrive by 13:00 for the drinks reception. The ceremony begins at 14:00 sharp, so we recommend arriving with a few minutes to spare to find your seat and settle in.

What's the dress code?

We'd love you to dress smartly for our Scottish celebration! Think cocktail attire or semi-formal. Ladies might consider wearing a fascinator or hat for the ceremony. Given it's October in Scotland, we recommend bringing a jacket or wrap.

What if it rains? This is Scotland after all!

We've planned for Scottish weather! All our venues have covered areas and the ceremony will be held in the chapel regardless of weather. Don't forget to bring an umbrella just in case for moving between venues.

Can I take photos during the ceremony?

We'd prefer an unplugged ceremony so everyone can be fully present for our vows. Our photographer will capture all the special moments to share with you later. Feel free to take photos during the reception and party!

Where exactly is Branxholm Park House?

Branxholm Park House
Hawick TD9 0JT, Scotland
The venue is located about 3 miles south of Hawick town center on the A7. Look for the grand stone gates and tree-lined driveway.

Is parking available at the venue?

Yes! There's ample free parking available on the grounds of Branxholm Park House. Follow the signs to the designated parking areas when you arrive.

Are all areas wheelchair accessible?

The main areas we'll be using are wheelchair accessible, including the chapel, orangery, and main house. If you have specific accessibility needs, please let us know when you RSVP so we can ensure everything is arranged for you.

What are the different venues being used throughout the day?

13:00 - Arrival: Main House (drinks reception)
14:00 - Ceremony: Wedding Chapel
15:00 - Reception: Rose Garden & Loggia
16:00 - Dinner: Belle Époque Orangery
19:00 - Party: Grand Ballroom & Garden Terrace

How do I get to Hawick?

By Car: Hawick is on the A7, about 1 hour from Edinburgh and 45 minutes from Carlisle.
By Train: No direct rail service to Hawick. Closest stations are Carlisle (45 min drive) or Edinburgh (1 hour drive).
By Air: Edinburgh Airport (1 hour drive) or Newcastle Airport (1.5 hours drive).

Where should I stay overnight?

We recommend booking accommodation early as it's a popular area. Some options include:
In Hawick: Mansfield House Hotel, Elm Court Hotel
Nearby: Kirklands Hotel (Kelso), The Cross Keys (Kelso)
B&Bs: Several charming local bed & breakfasts are available

Is there transport between venues?

All events take place at Branxholm Park House, so no transport between venues is needed! Just a pleasant stroll around the beautiful grounds between different areas of the estate.

What should I know about driving in the Scottish Borders?

The roads are generally good, but can be narrow and winding in places. Allow extra time for your journey, especially if you're unfamiliar with the area. GPS postcodes work well: TD9 0JT

What menu options will be available?

Starters: Choice of leek & potato soup or baked Camembert
Mains: British Beef Wellington, chicken with mozzarella & parma ham, or gnocchi with mushroom ragu
Dessert: Wedding cake and cheese boards served later in the evening

Can you accommodate dietary restrictions?

Absolutely! We have vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and lactose-free options available. Please let us know about any dietary requirements when you RSVP so we can ensure the kitchen is prepared.

What time is dinner served?

We'll be seated for dinner at 16:00 in the Belle Époque Orangery. Speeches will take place between the starter and main course. The evening will then continue with dancing and informal desserts.

Is there a bar service?

Yes! We'll have a full open bar available throughout the celebration, starting with the arrival drinks at 13:00 and continuing through the evening party until midnight.

Do you have a wedding registry?

Your presence at our wedding is the greatest gift of all! If you'd like to contribute to our honeymoon fund, you can find the details on our RSVP page.

Are cash gifts appropriate?

Your presence at our wedding is the greatest gift! If you'd like to give something, cash gifts are completely appropriate and very much appreciated as we start our married life together.

What if I can't attend but want to send a gift?

That's very thoughtful! You can send gifts from our registry directly to us, or feel free to email us for our address if you'd prefer to send something directly.

Should I bring gifts to the wedding?

While not necessary, if you'd like to bring a gift or card to the wedding, we'll have a designated table set up during the reception. Alternatively, having gifts sent directly to our home is perfectly fine too!

When is the RSVP deadline?

Please respond by September 1st, 2026. This gives us time to finalize catering numbers and seating arrangements. Earlier is even better if you know your plans!

Can I bring a plus-one?

Plus-ones are indicated on your invitation. If your invitation is addressed to you "and guest" or includes your partner's name, then yes! If you're unsure, please feel free to ask us.

How do I make changes after I RSVP?

Life happens! If your plans change after you've already responded, just send us an email as soon as possible and we'll update our records.
